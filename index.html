Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions clients most often bring to a first consultation.

What should I do immediately after being arrested in Indiana?

Stay calm, exercise your right to remain silent, and politely ask for an attorney. Do not discuss the facts of your case with police, cellmates, or family members on recorded jail phone lines. Call our office as soon as possible — we offer after-hours response for urgent criminal matters.

How long does an uncontested divorce take in Indiana?

Indiana imposes a statutory 60-day waiting period between filing and finalization. Truly uncontested cases — where both spouses agree on property, support, and parenting — can finalize close to that minimum. Cases with disputed issues typically take longer.

Can I get my Indiana criminal record expunged?

Many Indiana convictions are eligible for expungement after a waiting period (typically five to eight years for misdemeanors and Level 6 felonies, longer for more serious offenses). Eligibility depends on the specific offense, your record since, and whether you meet statutory requirements. We can assess eligibility during a free consultation.

What does a DUI/OWI cost in Indiana?

Beyond legal fees, a first OWI conviction in Indiana commonly involves court costs, fines, license suspension, increased insurance, possible ignition interlock, and required programs — often $5,000 to $10,000+ in total impact. Outcomes vary widely based on the facts, prior history, and how the case is handled, which is why early counsel matters.
